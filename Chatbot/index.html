Disease Awareness

Learn about common diseases, their symptoms, causes, and prevention strategies.

Dengue
Mosquito-borne viral infection

Symptoms: High fever, severe headache, pain behind the eyes, joint and muscle pain, nausea, skin rash.

Causes: Spread through the bite of infected Aedes mosquitoes.

Prevention: Avoid mosquito bites, remove stagnant water, use mosquito nets and repellents.

Malaria
Parasitic mosquito-borne disease

Symptoms: Fever with chills, sweating, headache, nausea, vomiting, fatigue.

Causes: Caused by Plasmodium parasites transmitted through Anopheles mosquitoes.

Prevention: Use mosquito nets, repellents, indoor spraying, and follow local health guidance.

COVID-19
Respiratory viral infection

Symptoms: Fever, dry cough, tiredness, loss of taste or smell, sore throat, difficulty breathing.

Causes: Caused by SARS-CoV-2 virus, spread via respiratory droplets and aerosols.

Prevention: Vaccination, masks in crowded places, hand hygiene, good ventilation, physical distancing.

Diabetes
Chronic metabolic disorder

Symptoms: Increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision, slow-healing sores.

Causes: Insufficient insulin production or ineffective insulin use; influenced by genetics and lifestyle.

Prevention: Healthy diet, regular exercise, weight management, routine health check-ups.

Influenza (Flu)
Seasonal viral infection

Symptoms: Sudden fever, chills, cough, sore throat, muscle aches, fatigue.

Causes: Infection with influenza viruses, spread through droplets when people cough or sneeze.

Prevention: Annual flu vaccination, hand hygiene, covering coughs and sneezes, staying home when sick.

Prevention Tips

Small daily actions can make a big difference in protecting your health and community.

Hand Hygiene

Wash hands regularly with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially before eating and after contact with public surfaces.

Vaccination Awareness

Stay informed about recommended vaccines for your age and region, and follow your local health authority's guidelines.

Mosquito Protection

Remove stagnant water around your home, use nets and repellents, and wear long-sleeved clothing in mosquito-prone areas.

Healthy Lifestyle

Eat a balanced diet, exercise regularly, sleep well, and manage stress to strengthen your immune system.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common health awareness questions.

If you develop a fever, monitor your temperature, stay hydrated, rest, and avoid self-medication with antibiotics. Seek medical advice if the fever persists, becomes very high, or is accompanied by warning signs such as difficulty breathing, chest pain, severe headache, rash, or confusion.

Dengue spreads through the bite of infected Aedes mosquitoes, which usually bite during the day. The virus is not spread directly from person to person. Preventing mosquito breeding and avoiding mosquito bites are key strategies to reduce dengue transmission.

You should consult a doctor if your symptoms are severe, persistent, or worsening; if you have difficulty breathing, chest pain, confusion, or persistent high fever; or if you belong to a higher-risk group such as young children, older adults, or people with chronic health conditions.
